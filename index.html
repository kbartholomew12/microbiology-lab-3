Positive staining procedure
Check off each step as you complete it. All steps must be confirmed before moving on.
Preparing a positive stain of your organism using your stain.
  1. 1
    Using your water bottle, place a single drop of water on your inoculating loop and transfer it to the center of the slide.
  2. 2
    Transfer a small amount of bacteria into the drop and swirl in circular motion to create a large smear.
  3. 3
    Spread the smear wide enough so cells separate and the sample can air dry easily.
  4. 4
    Heat-fix the slide by quickly passing it through the flame 2–3 times. Use a clothespin to hold the slide.
  5. 5
    Place slide on staining tray. Add your stain and let it stand for 1 minute.
  6. 6
    Hold slide at a 45° angle and wash with water bottle to remove excess stain.
  7. 7
    Blot dry using bibulous paper — gently press, do not wipe.
  8. 8
    Add immersion oil and view at the 100× oil-immersion objective.

Positive vs. negative staining — comparison
Positive staining

Stain used: Cationic (methylene blue, crystal violet, safranin)

What stains: The cells

Background: Clear

Heat-fix required: Yes

Best for: Viewing shape & arrangement

Negative staining

Stain used: Anionic (nigrosin)

What stains: The background

Background: Dark gray/black

Heat-fix required: No

Best for: Accurate cell size measurement

Negative staining procedure
Now perform the negative stain on the same organism. Check off each step.
Using nigrosin (anionic stain) on your organism. Remember: no heat-fixing!
  1. 1
    Place a single drop of nigrosin towards the short end of a clean glass slide.
  2. 2
    Transfer a small amount of bacteria directly into the nigrosin drop using the inoculating loop.
  3. 3
    Using a second slide at 45°, drag-smear the bacteria and stain in one continuous motion along the full slide length.
  4. 4
    Air dry completely. Do NOT heat-fix. View at 100× with oil immersion.
  5. 5
    Discard both slides in the sharps container.
  6. 6
    Clean each objective lens with lens cleaning paper and solution. Return microscope for checkout.
